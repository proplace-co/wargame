Market & Timing
The SEPA Verification of Payee market is structurally consolidating: initially fragmented across many specialist providers, the October 2025 EU Instant Payments Regulation has now created the conditions for mandatory aggregation, where compliance-layer specialists either establish market leadership quickly or get absorbed into incumbent bundles. The timing verdict is 'perfect window' — the October 2025 EU Instant Payments Regulation acted as the definitive hard-deadline catalyst for adoption, converting CheckPayee's product from a nice-to-have fraud prevention tool into a legal obligation for every SEPA payments participant, thereby eliminating the discretionary budget approval step that typically adds 6 to 12 months to enterprise fintech sales cycles. The primary timing catalyst is the non-discretionary nature of the VoP mandate: compliance officers at commercial banks and PSPs face regulatory penalties for non-compliance, which means the procurement conversation starts from obligation rather than from business-case justification. The window for first-mover advantage in new geographies such as France and Benelux is contracting, and the risk that incumbent payment infrastructure providers bundle VoP natively — cited as potentially materializing as early as late 2024 — is now a real observed market dynamic rather than a future risk.

Competitive Advantage
CheckPayee's primary moat is regulatory-induced market entrenchment: the company has embedded a compliance-critical API into the mission-critical payment processing flows of 50-plus financial institutions, creating switching costs that are not merely commercial but regulatory — removing the API requires re-certification, re-integration, and regulatory re-approval of the bank's payment processing architecture. The moat strength is assessed as moderate rather than strong because it is built on a narrow regulatory perimeter (SEPA VoP) that large incumbents can approach from a platform bundling angle rather than a direct technical competition angle. The competitive mechanism is specialization and speed of deployment: CheckPayee goes live in 2 to 4 weeks with a matching engine (Levenshtein-and-phonetics-based) that outperforms generic string comparison in accuracy, which creates a quality differential that deters migration even when a bundled alternative becomes available. The build-versus-acquire verdict for a potential strategic acquirer is 'must acquire' — replicating the proven accuracy, sub-one-second performance, and the 50-plus institutional trust relationships within the SEPA regulatory context takes years of domain-specific R&D that cannot be compressed by budget. The primary competitive threats are major incumbent payment infrastructure providers (SWIFT, Vocalink, Temenos) at medium response speed and large commercial banks with internal development capability at medium response speed — CheckPayee's defense against both is specialized execution velocity and the institutional knowledge embedded in its integration codebase. OFFRE BASSE — pourquoi payer moins

▸ Pourquoi

▸ Tactiques de négociation
  • Liquidation Preference 2x non-participating senior : à l'exit, le fonds récupère 2× son ticket avant tout autre actionnaire — protection downside justifiée par l'absence d'audit de l'ARR et le risque d'execution.
  • Anti-dilution full ratchet pour 18-24 mois sur la prochaine série : protège intégralement le fonds en cas de down-round (resize tickets précédents au nouveau prix). Coût pour le founder mais cohérent avec une entrée valorisation basse.
  • 1 board seat lead investor + droits de veto étendus (budget annuel, hire/fire CEO ou CFO, M&A, dette > €1M, equity plan modification, related-party). Compense l'incertitude sur l'exécution par un contrôle renforcé du board.
  • Super pro-rata + ROFR sur la prochaine série (1.5× à 2× la part actuelle) : permet de doubler la mise sans dilution si la traction se confirme, sans s'engager si elle ne se confirme pas.

OFFRE JUSTE — pourquoi rester ici

▸ Pourquoi
  • La valeur retenue (€16.0M) s'appuie sur le multiple de revenu (profil croissance) — la lentille adaptée au profil de la cible, pas une moyenne aveugle de méthodes qui mesurent des choses différentes. C'est l'ancrage défendable face à un acquéreur tiers pour cadrer la zone d'atterrissage.
  • Le multiple de revenu valorise la cible à €16.0M (croissance), mais sa génération de cash (EBITDA normalisé/DCF) ne soutient que €5.5M — soit 2.9× moins. ▸ Tactiques de négociation
  • Liquidation Preference 1x non-participating (standard pré-Series B) : le fonds récupère son ticket à l'exit, le surplus se partage au pro-rata. Pricing standard du marché — pas de levier downside additionnel.
  • Pro-rata rights sur la prochaine série au prix retenu : le fonds garde la possibilité de maintenir son ownership sans engagement obligatoire. Option valorisée mais sans coût immédiat.
  • 1 board seat + information rights mensuels (revenue, cash, headcount, MRR, burn). Visibilité standard du marché pour la lead VC, sans veto étendu sur les décisions opérationnelles courantes.
  • Anti-dilution weighted average broad-based + drag-along 60-75% du capital. Protection downside standard sans pénaliser excessivement les founders.
