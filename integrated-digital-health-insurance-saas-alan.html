They target advanced AI underwriting in 'Stage 3'. Strengths are financial firepower; weaknesses are legacy systems.

2. THE ASPIRANTS: Low Capacity • Active Posture
Known as 'Climbers', these T4 ScaleUps are aggressive and opportunistic (Getsafe, Plum) or defensive Fortresses (Coya). They operate in 'Stage 3' and 'Stage 6' and often pursue roll-up strategies or alliances, but face threats from larger 'Hunters'.

3. THE GIANTS: High Capacity • Passive Posture
These 'Sleeping Giants' (e.g., Aetna) have extensive capital but a passive M&A posture. As a subsidiary of CVS Health, Aetna is a 'Hunted' target rather than an active acquirer, despite its 'Stage 3' AI platform capabilities. Its fate depends on its parent company's strategy.

4. THE POTENTIAL TARGETS: Low Capacity • Passive Posture
These are prime 'Targets' or 'Partners', typically 'Hunted' or 'Distressed' T4 ScaleUps (Alan, Wefox, Ottonova, etc.). They are attractive for their innovative solutions, customer bases, and specialized tech in 'Stage 3' and 'Stage 6', making them M&A targets for larger companies filling strategic gaps.
