Market DEFINITION

Operations leaders at large enterprises are purchasing 'agentic automation' software to execute complex, multi-step back-office workflows. The specific job is to delegate cognitive tasks previously requiring human judgment, like exception handling in accounts payable or candidate screening in HR, to an AI system. The current market is broken because legacy Robotic Process Automation (RPA) is brittle, failing when user interfaces or processes change, while hiring more people is prohibitively expensive and unscalable.

This market sits downstream from foundational AI model providers (like OpenAI) and upstream of enterprise systems of record (like SAP/Salesforce), positioning itself as the new orchestration layer that captures value by becoming the 'brain' for all business processes.

Our Market THESIS

The widespread availability of high-fidelity LLMs has permanently broken the assumption that automation must be rules-based, opening the door for systems that can reason and adapt. Incumbents like UiPath cannot fully embrace this dynamic without cannibalizing their existing, high-margin RPA business and re-architecting their entire platform, creating a window of opportunity. The winning attack vector is not a better chatbot, but a 'headless' agent that reliably executes mission-critical back-office jobs, starting with the high-pain, high-value workflows in finance and HR that are too complex for legacy tools.

This window is open now because enterprises are actively seeking AI solutions beyond pilots, but it will begin to close within 24 months as the market consolidates around a few platforms that achieve true workflow reliability and demonstrate undeniable ROI.

Our CONVICTION & WAGER on this Market:

HIGH CONVICTION

While this market is dangerously crowded with both incumbents and a flood of new startups, the sheer scale of the value creation from automating cognitive labor makes it impossible to ignore. Our conviction rests on the belief that true 'self-learning' technology will create a winner-take-all dynamic, separating one or two generational companies from a graveyard of 'good enough' tools.

Our wager is that enterprise buyers will, within the next 18 months, shift their purchasing criteria from the number of features to a single metric: the percentage of workflows completed autonomously with zero human exceptions. The first call signal that raises our conviction is a founder who can answer, 'What is the most complex, multi-step, multi-system workflow your agent has ever completed entirely on its own, and what specific technical insight allowed you to solve for the edge cases where every other system fails?'
