DÉFINITION du Marché

Les logiciels et systèmes intégrés de surveillance périmétrique et de défense anti-drone (C-UAV) pour infrastructures critiques industrielles et gouvernementales s\'adressent aux directeurs de la sécurité des sites d\'importance nationale supérieure. Ces acheteurs acquièrent des systèmes logiciels d\'unification pour régler le problème critique de la détection précoce, tridimensionnelle et sans faille des menaces d\'intrusion terrestres et aériennes.

Ce marché est actuellement structuré par un amont composé de fabricants de matériels spécifiques (radars compacts, caméras thermiques) et un aval dominé par des intégrateurs globaux, laissant la zone intermédiaire - celle de l\'intelligence logicielle de fusion - comme le principal pool de valeur à haute marge.

Notre THÈSE de Marché

La vulnérabilité physique des infrastructures critiques européennes est devenue une réalité géopolitique indéniable, matérialisée par la recrudescence de menaces asymétriques telles que les survols de drones malveillants sur les sites nucléaires et le sabotage industriel. Les acteurs historiques de la surveillance physique sont paralysés par leur modèle de distribution centré sur le matériel, ce qui les empêche de développer des moteurs de fusion logicielle agnostiques performants sans froisser leurs accords technologiques exclusifs.

L\'angle d\'attaque gagnant consiste à s\'interposer comme la console d\'unification logicielle open-platform par laquelle transitent obligatoirement toutes les données des capteurs tiers. La fenêtre d\'opportunité pour standardiser ces plateformes de commande est ouverte immédiatement en raison du durcissement des directives de sécurité européennes NIS 2, et se refermera à mesure que les grands intégrateurs consolideront leurs propres outils logiciels propriétaires.

Évaluations structurelles

Market & Timing

The perimeter security market in which Securify operates is in active consolidation: it is fragmented at the hardware and local-integrator layer but structurally concentrating at the software middleware layer, where platform scale creates compounding switching cost advantages.

Securify's CoreCommand occupies this middleware position in the Nordic institutional tier, where it has displaced the alternative of client-managed siloed sensor networks with a unified volumetric command architecture. The timing verdict is optimal right now: the NIS2 directive entered full enforcement in October 2024, creating a compliance mandate for critical infrastructure operators to demonstrate unified physical-digital security event logging — a requirement that CoreCommand fulfils and that standalone hardware configurations cannot meet.

The primary timing catalyst is therefore regulatory rather than technological: the demand is not created by innovation but mandated by law, which makes it more durable and less subject to demand-side reversal. The primary timing risk is the competitive response from Genetec and Milestone, both of which have the engineering resources to develop native multi-sensor fusion modules.

The 12-18 month response window for these incumbents is credible, which means Prosero must close and begin KameraPortalen integration work before the end of Q1 2027 to establish a combined moat that is defensible against commodity platform responses.

Competitive Advantage

Securify's primary moat is switching costs generated by the depth of CoreCommand's integration into client PSIM and VMS systems: once the software is hard-coded into the operational security dashboard of a prison or an airport, replacement requires a full re-certification project that carries both technical risk and regulatory exposure for the client operator.

This moat is rated moderate rather than strong because it depends on partner distribution consistency and does not benefit from patent protection — the proprietary logic for multi-sensor filtering is held as trade secrets, which protects against reverse engineering but does not provide the legal exclusivity of defensive patents.

The mechanism by which the moat self-reinforces is the 'Securify-Certified' partner training barrier: each new integrator partner requires 3-6 months of intensive field training before achieving certified-support status, creating a network of technically committed distribution partners who are economically motivated to protect the CoreCommand ecosystem.

The build versus acquire verdict is unambiguous: the 5-7 year R&D cycle required to achieve agency-grade security certifications and replicate the heterogeneous sensor integration libraries exceeds any realistic internal development budget for Prosero.

The primary competitive threat is Genetec pivoting its VMS into native fusion capabilities, which is fast and credible. The secondary threat is dedicated C-UAV players developing high-end integrative software stacks, which is medium-term.

Hardware vendors building internal software fusion layers is a slow and structurally self-defeating move that would require them to invalidate their own closed-ecosystem hardware business model. The moat collapses if Genetec or Milestone release native, high-performance multi-sensor correlation modules before Prosero completes the KameraPortalen integration — this is the single scenario that converts STRONG_FIT into WEAK_FIT within 24 months.

Founder, Financial Health & Hidden Risks

Kenneth Nyström has led Securify since 2014 without external capital, accumulating over a decade of domain-specific expertise in sensor interoperability for institutional perimeter security. His earned insight — that integration latency at the fusion layer, not hardware sensor performance, is the primary value driver for infrastructure operators — is the kind of non-obvious domain knowledge that explains why a 5-15 person team holds frame agreements with sovereign-grade clients that global security conglomerates have not displaced.

Execution evidence is strong: successful deployments with Kriminalvården and Swedavia confirm the ability to meet high-grade institutional regulatory and technical demands, and the 12-year organic growth trajectory without venture dilution demonstrates capital efficiency.

The anti-pattern detected is founder dependency: the business relies heavily on Nyström's technical leadership and the 'Proud Nerds' engineering culture, and integration into a large, process-heavy group like Prosero carries a confirmed risk of losing the intellectual capital and engineering velocity that currently defines the product.

Analyst 3 documented a specific contradiction: Nyström expressed an explicit preference for building over buying and showed skepticism toward M&A-driven geographic consolidation, which means the founder may function as an internal friction point during Prosero integration rather than as a catalyst.

Financial health is largely inferred: gross margin quality is undocumented, burn multiple is undisclosed, revenue concentration is unverified, and the path to profitability is confirmed qualitatively (stable SME, no burn signals) but not audited.

The hidden risk is hardware obsolescence within the integrator channel — if radar and C-UAV manufacturers begin offering bundled software management consoles as free value-adds to drive hardware sales, Securify may face pressure to commoditize CoreCommand pricing to retain integrator loyalty, eroding the software margin that underpins the entire thesis.

The overlooked factor that changes the valuation frame is the middleware tax mechanism: by forcing hardware vendors to conform to the CoreCommand API, Securify has permanently shifted the control point to the software layer, which means Prosero would acquire not just recurring SMA revenue but the right to define which hardware vendors are commercially viable across any campus in its network — a supplier leverage capability that has significant long-term margin implications beyond the face value of the SMA line.

Devil's Advocate

The synthesis treats CoreCommand's technical complexity as direct evidence of commercial defensibility — but these are not the same thing. Technical lock-in (the cost of re-integrating a sensor network) is high only if the client operator actively values the integration.

The synthesis does not model this scenario's probability or its financial impact on the KameraPortalen integration synergy.

The claim that Securify will scale from 2 major projects per year to 15+ using Prosero's technician fleet assumes that Prosero's 2,000 field technicians can be trained to install and certify sovereign-grade multi-sensor fusion systems.

In this scenario, Prosero has paid a premium software multiple for an asset that has reverted to project-services economics within 24 months of closing, with the KameraPortalen integration incomplete and no sovereign frame agreement leverage remaining.

This is not a probable scenario but it is not a tail scenario either — each of its three components has individually confirmed evidence. Devil's verdict: the CALL is strategically justified but the conviction is Medium, not High — the financial audit and the founder covenant are not formalities, they are the two binary conditions that determine whether this is a platform acquisition or an expensive talent acqui-hire.
